## Risk Description

Enterprise organizations move slowly. Even warm-network outreach can take 4–8 weeks from first conversation to signed contract. Cold outreach to mid-market enterprise buyers can take 3–6 months. This creates a pre-revenue window during which the company is building infrastructure and positioning but not generating income.

## Severity Assessment

**Medium.** The Chairman is the sole investor and has funded the company. The question is whether the founding team should recommend strategies to accelerate first revenue, or accept the pre-revenue window as a necessary feature of the positioning strategy.

Accepting low-value work (small freelance gigs, content projects, consumer UX) to bridge cash flow would risk positioning dilution. Rejecting all non-ideal work risks extending the pre-revenue window past the Chairman's comfort zone.

## Mitigation Strategies

1. **Prioritize add-on services as early revenue.** UX audits ($2,500–$4,500) and workshop facilitation ($1,500–$2,500/day) have faster sales cycles than full engagements. They can be sold to warm-network contacts without a full procurement process.

2. **Target mid-market SaaS companies for first engagements.** Enterprise SaaS companies (50–500 employees) have faster decision-making than large enterprises. They're an ideal first client segment.

3. **Use warm-network outreach exclusively in Month 1–2.** Former colleagues, referrals from ThoughtWorks network, and Safrapay-era contacts are the fastest path to first revenue.

4. **Avoid discounting the Discovery Sprint for revenue acceleration.** A discounted first Sprint sets a pricing expectation that is hard to reset. If a relationship requires pricing flexibility, offer a payment structure change rather than a rate cut.

5. **Set a cash flow review trigger.** If no paid work is contracted by Month 3, the Chairman should be informed and a strategy review should occur.

## What Would Resolve This Risk

First signed Discovery Sprint contract. Once one engagement is running, the risk converts to a standard business operations challenge rather than an existential pre-revenue risk.
