## Risk Description

AI Experience Design is a legitimate and growing practice area. The Chairman holds an NNG AI Experience Design certification and has ThoughtWorks AI initiative experience. However, the Allfer Consulting delivery record in AI product design is currently zero.

Positioning AI product design as a core capability pillar before completing AI-specific engagements creates a risk of overpromising to clients who specifically need AI UX expertise and discovering gaps in delivery.

## Severity Assessment

**Low.** The Chairman's credentials are real. NNG certification is a respected signal of AI UX competence. The risk is one of degree, not authenticity. The key mitigation is ensuring that the AI capability is framed as strategic design and advisory (which the Chairman can deliver confidently) rather than as hands-on AI engineering or ML pipeline design (which is not the practice).

## Mitigation Strategies

1. **Frame AI capability as interaction and experience design, not AI engineering.** The practice is: how do humans experience AI-powered products? Not: how do you build AI systems?

2. **Lead with AI feature consulting (hourly, $175–$225) rather than AI transformation engagements for first AI-specific work.** Lower stakes, faster delivery, and builds the delivery record.

3. **Do not claim case studies in AI product design until one exists under Allfer.** Prior ThoughtWorks AI work can be cited as background, not as Allfer case studies.

4. **Publish the AI Product Experience Design Guidelines as an IP asset.** This demonstrates rigor and frames the practice's scope clearly — before a client engagement puts it to the test.

Revisit after the first AI feature consulting engagement is complete.
