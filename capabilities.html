Capabilities

Four pillars. One practice.

Each capability is grounded in real client work. Together they form a practice that handles complexity, fragmentation, and high-stakes design that generalist agencies cannot.

Pillar 01

Enterprise UX & Complex Systems Design

Core Offering

Multi-surface, multi-stakeholder digital products designed for the people who use them every day — not just for the demo. This includes B2B SaaS, internal tools, frontline worker applications, and enterprise platforms with high workflow complexity.

The core challenge in enterprise UX is not execution — it's coordination. Multiple teams, conflicting mental models, years of accumulated design debt, and a user base that cannot be replaced. This practice is built around those constraints.

B2B SaaS Internal tools Frontline worker apps Multi-stakeholder design Workflow complexity
Supporting Experience

First Student: designed Driver mobile app + web portal for tens of thousands of frontline workers, owned the onboarding domain end-to-end across multiple surfaces. Kroger: enterprise UX engagement through ThoughtWorks navigating complex stakeholder environments at scale. ThoughtWorks: senior UX consultant across cross-industry transformation engagements.

Pillar 02

Service Design for Platform Coherence

Core Offering

Mapping the full service ecosystem to close the gap between how organizations are structured internally and how users experience them across touchpoints. The core offer for multi-location enterprises, platform fragmentation, and cross-team alignment problems.

Most enterprise fragmentation is not a design problem. It's a coordination and governance problem that manifests as a design problem. Service design provides the methodology to name it, map it, and fix it in a way that survives organizational change.

Service blueprinting Journey mapping Platform coherence Cross-team alignment Ecosystem audits
Pillar 03

Regulated & Compliance-Aware UX

Differentiating Capability

Designing financial, healthcare, and enterprise products within compliance constraints without sacrificing usability. Rooted in the Chairman's fintech and banking experience at Safrapay. This is the capability that separates Allfer Consulting from design generalists in high-stakes industries.

Regulated UX is not about adding disclaimers. It's about understanding how compliance requirements shape information architecture, disclosure patterns, error handling, and user flows — and designing within those constraints in ways that still feel human.

Fintech & banking Compliance-aware IA ISO-compliant portals Regulated disclosure UX Accessibility
Supporting Experience

Safrapay: UX Designer to UX Team Lead. Led design of a B2B SaaS ISO Portal and coordinated the company's first banking app launch. Deep experience in regulated financial product design, compliance-aware UX, and fintech workflows.

Pillar 04

AI Product & Transformation Design

Emerging Capability

Designing for the human side of AI-powered products — the handoffs, the trust moments, the edge cases, and the workflows that automation doesn't cover. Grounded in NNG AI Experience Design certification and ThoughtWorks AI initiative experience.

The biggest failure mode in AI product design is treating AI as a feature rather than a new class of actor in the workflow. This practice addresses AI as a design problem: how do users understand it, trust it, correct it, and remain in control of it?

AI UX design Human-AI handoff design Trust & transparency patterns AI workflow design NNG certified
