## The Decision

The company will lead with **enterprise complexity and service coherence** as its primary market wedge. This means all positioning, pitch language, and first-contact framing emphasizes the business cost of fragmented enterprise experiences — not design process, not deliverables, not methodology.

## What Was Evaluated

**Option A: Enterprise complexity as wedge**
Narrows the ICP to organizations with genuinely complex, multi-surface digital products. Separates Allfer from generalists and freelancers who compete on execution. Strong match for Chairman's background across fintech, logistics, and ThoughtWorks engagements.

**Option B: Service design as primary hook**
Positions service design as the lead capability. More methodologically differentiated but harder to explain to non-design-literate enterprise buyers. Risk: perceived as a process/facilitation vendor rather than a strategic partner.

**Option C: Regulated UX as primary hook**
Strongest credibility match (Safrapay). Narrows the market too severely for company formation phase. Better as a proof point than a wedge.

## Why This Option

- Enterprise complexity is underserved at the mid-market: large consultancies over-resource it, freelancers under-resource it
- The Chairman's background authentically spans fintech, logistics, and B2B SaaS at scale — all enterprise-complexity contexts
- "Fragmented experience" is a pain enterprise buyers can name and quantify (onboarding errors, support tickets, staff confusion)
- The wedge is defensible: it requires experience, methodology, and systems-thinking that generalist designers cannot fake
- Pricing leverage is real: complexity-specific expertise commands senior consultant rates without justification

## How This Changes Positioning

All client-facing language now leads with business impact, not design process. The pitch hook names the operational cost of fragmentation before mentioning UX or design. Service design, regulated UX, and AI product design are positioned as the methodological proof behind the wedge — not as separate service categories.

## Assumptions

- Enterprise mid-market buyers can recognize and describe their fragmentation pain without being educated on service design first
- The Chairman's background is sufficient to own this positioning without overstretching
- Discovery Sprint is the right entry offer because it resolves the fragmentation question before proposing the solution
