## What it is

A Discovery Sprint is a bounded, time-limited engagement designed for organizations that know something is broken — but haven't defined the problem well enough to know what to fix.

In 3–4 weeks, we map your current experience ecosystem, talk to the people who use your platforms every day, and deliver a clear picture of what's fragmented, what's working, and what to address first.

## Who it's for

- Organizations adding new features to platforms that already feel incoherent
- Companies that have heard "the app is confusing" from users but don't know why
- Teams preparing to redesign a product and want to make sure they're solving the right problem
- Organizations evaluating whether to rebuild or evolve a legacy platform
- Companies adding AI features to existing workflows and needing to understand the design implications first

## What you get

By the end of a Discovery Sprint, you will have:

1. **A service ecosystem audit** — a structured view of how your platforms are connected, where the gaps are, and what users experience across the full journey
2. **Research findings** — synthesized insights from 5–8 stakeholder or user interviews
3. **Cross-platform journey maps** — visual documentation of the experience as it actually works (not as it was designed to work)
4. **A design direction report** — clear findings, a validated problem definition, and specific recommendations
5. **A prioritized roadmap** — ranked by impact and feasibility, scoped for the next phase of work

## What happens next

Most clients move to a Project Engagement after a Discovery Sprint. The Sprint defines the scope; the Project delivers the solution. Some clients take the Sprint output internally and act on it with their own team — that's also a valid outcome.
