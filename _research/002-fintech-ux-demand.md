## Finding

Fintech and regulated financial products are the strongest near-term market for Allfer Consulting. The Chairman's background at Safrapay provides authentic credibility that cannot be easily matched by generalist agencies. Enterprise buyers in this space pay premium rates and respond to demonstrated domain expertise.

## Why Fintech Is the Right Entry Market

**Compliance creates a skills floor generalists can't clear.** Designing a B2B SaaS ISO portal, a banking app onboarding flow, or a regulated disclosure screen requires understanding of both UX principles and compliance constraints. Most UX designers have neither. The Chairman has both.

**The cost of poor UX is quantifiable in financial products.** Failed onboarding = lost account. Confusing disclosure = regulatory risk. Slow workflow = operational cost. Enterprise buyers in fintech can attach dollar values to UX problems, which makes it easier to justify consultant rates.

**Mid-size fintech companies are underserved.** Seed-stage fintechs use freelancers or build in-house. Series C and above can afford embedded design teams. The mid-market (Series A–B, $5M–$50M ARR) often has a product team but no senior UX expertise — and is building regulated products fast.

**AI is entering fintech.** Fraud detection UX, AI-powered credit decisioning interfaces, and automated advisory tools all require AI experience design expertise. This intersects directly with Allfer's fourth capability pillar.

## Credibility Signal

The Chairman's Safrapay work — including the B2B SaaS ISO Portal and the first banking app launch — is the single strongest proof point in this market. No competing generalist agency can replicate this background authentically.

## Outreach Implication

Initial outreach should prioritize Miami-area fintech companies, Florida-based banks and credit unions undergoing digital transformation, and Series A–B fintech companies nationally with visible UX or product job postings (a signal that they're investing in product quality).
