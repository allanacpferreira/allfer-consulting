## Finding

Enterprise organizations with genuinely complex digital products — multi-platform, multi-stakeholder, regulated, or undergoing AI transformation — are poorly served by the current consultant landscape. The gap between freelance UX designers and Big 4 design arms is wide, expensive to cross, and exactly where Allfer Consulting operates.

## The Landscape

**Large consultancies (Accenture Interactive, McKinsey Design, Deloitte Digital)**
- Strong brand and enterprise relationships
- Expensive: senior design roles billed at $300–$500/hour
- Clients often get junior or mid-level designers at senior rates
- Methodology is standardized, not tailored to specific industry complexity
- Minimum engagement sizes typically exclude mid-market companies

**Boutique design agencies (20–100 person firms)**
- More specialized but often skew toward product/consumer work
- Enterprise UX with compliance or regulated-industry depth is uncommon
- Engagement models tend toward fixed-scope project work, not embedded strategy
- Most lack genuine service design methodology (use the term loosely)

**Freelancers and independent consultants**
- Abundant at $75–$150/hour
- Rarely bring service design, multi-platform, or compliance-aware expertise
- No institutional continuity — each engagement starts from zero
- Not credible to enterprise procurement or legal for larger engagements

**The gap:** A senior-level practitioner with real enterprise experience, a documented methodology, and compliance/AI depth — who operates at boutique rates and delivers consultant-level strategic thinking. This is Allfer.

## Demand Signals

- Enterprise software complexity is increasing, not decreasing. AI features, acquisitions, and platform consolidations are generating new UX coherence problems at scale.
- Fintech regulation (CFPB oversight, open banking, fraud UX requirements) is increasing the cost of poor design in financial products.
- Frontline worker tech investment is growing: logistics, healthcare, and retail are digitalizing frontline roles with apps that are frequently poorly designed.
- AI transformation is creating a new demand category: organizations adding AI features without understanding the interaction design implications.

## Assumptions

- Mid-market enterprise buyers ($50M–$500M revenue) have budget for senior consultant rates but cannot access Big 4 firms cost-effectively
- Procurement gatekeeping is the main barrier — will require a clear, credentialed proposal format to pass legal/procurement review
- The Discovery Sprint is the right entry offer for buyers who cannot justify a $35,000 engagement without proof of fit

## Implications for Allfer

- The positioning wedge is real and occupiable without overstretching
- Fintech and financial services are the highest-value entry market given the Chairman's background
- Enterprise SaaS mid-scale-up is the highest-volume market with the fastest sales cycle
- The company does not need to compete on price — it needs to compete on credibility and specificity
